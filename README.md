## Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of **British Airways (BA) customer reviews** from 2017 to 2023, utilizing advanced data visualization and business intelligence techniques. The objective is to identify key trends in passenger satisfaction across various service dimensions, aircraft types, and traveler categories. The insights are visualized through both a **live interactive website dashboard** and a **Tableau Public dashboard**, providing multiple perspectives on BA's service quality.

## Key Insights & Findings

The analysis reveals several critical aspects of the passenger experience:

**Overall Performance**: The airline maintains an **Average Overall Rating of 4.2/10**, though specific service areas show significant room for improvement.

**Service Dimensions**: While **Cabin Staff Service (3.3/5)** and **Ground Service (3.0/5)** are rated moderately, **Seat Comfort (2.9/5)** and **Value for Money (2.8/5)** are the lowest-rated categories, indicating a need for cabin interior upgrades and pricing strategy reviews.

**Aircraft Performance**: Ratings vary significantly by aircraft. Modern wide-body aircraft like the **Boeing 787** and **Boeing 777** tend to receive higher satisfaction scores compared to older or narrow-body models.

**Temporal Trends**: Passenger satisfaction has shown notable fluctuations over the years (2017-2023), likely influenced by global travel conditions and internal service changes.

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategic recommendations are proposed:

**Cabin Refurbishment**: Prioritize seat comfort upgrades, especially on older aircraft models that consistently receive lower ratings. Modern aircraft like the 787 demonstrate the positive impact of contemporary cabin designs.

**Value Proposition**: Re-evaluate pricing and service inclusions to improve the perceived "Value for Money" score. Consider bundling services or offering tiered pricing options.

**Ground Service Consistency**: Streamline ground operations at major hubs like Heathrow to boost Ground Service ratings and reduce operational delays.
