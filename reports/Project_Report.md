# British Airways Review Data Analysis Project Report

## Executive Summary
This report details the findings from an in-depth data analysis project focusing on **British Airways (BA) customer reviews** from 2017 to 2023. The analysis aims to uncover key drivers of passenger satisfaction and areas requiring service improvement. The project utilizes a dataset of customer reviews and a supplementary countries dataset to provide a global perspective on BA's performance.

## Data Overview
The analysis is based on two primary datasets:
1.  **ba_reviews.csv**: Contains customer reviews, ratings (1-10), aircraft types, traveler types, seat types, and specific service scores (Cabin Staff, Ground Service, Seat Comfort, Value for Money).
2.  **Countries.csv**: Provides geographic mapping (Continent, Region) to enhance the spatial analysis of reviews.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
| Metric | Average Score |

| **Overall Rating** | **4.2 / 10** |

| **Value For Money** | **2.8 / 5** |

## Analysis & Insights

### 1. Service Quality Trends
The data reveals a consistent challenge in **Seat Comfort** and **Value for Money**, both falling below a 3.0 average. While **Cabin Staff Service** is the highest-rated dimension, it still suggests room for improvement in consistent service delivery across all routes.

### 2. Aircraft Performance
Horizontal bar charts in the dashboard show that wide-body aircraft like the **Boeing 787** and **Boeing 777** generally receive higher satisfaction scores compared to older narrow-body models like the **A320** and **A321**. This highlights the impact of modern cabin designs on passenger experience.

### 3. Geographic Review Distribution
The map visualization indicates a high volume of reviews from the **United Kingdom**, **United States**, and **Europe**. Ratings show regional variations, potentially reflecting different service standards on long-haul versus short-haul routes.

### 4. Temporal Analysis
The **Average Overall Rating By Month** shows significant volatility between 2017 and 2023. Sharp dips in ratings often coincide with periods of operational disruption or service cutbacks, while peaks suggest successful service enhancements or positive seasonal effects.

## Recommendations
- **Cabin Refurbishment**: Prioritize seat comfort upgrades, especially on older aircraft models that consistently receive lower ratings.
- **Value Proposition**: Re-evaluate pricing and service inclusions to improve the perceived "Value for Money" score.
- **Ground Service Consistency**: Streamline ground operations at major hubs like Heathrow to boost Ground Service ratings.

## Conclusion
The **British Airways Review Analysis** project demonstrates that while BA maintains a stable overall rating, targeted improvements in seat comfort and value perception are essential to compete effectively with other premium carriers. The interactive Tableau dashboard provides a powerful tool for ongoing monitoring of these critical service metrics.
